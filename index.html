First time bringing a car to an independent shop? Here's exactly what happens from your first call to picking up your keys — no surprises, no pressure.

Most customers are in and out the same day. For complex repairs or jobs that require ordered parts, we'll give you a realistic timeline upfront and keep you updated as the work progresses. We won't start anything without your approval.

Why It Matters

Why Choose an
Independent Shop?

Dealers and national chains have their place — but for most repairs, an independent shop like D's gives you something they can't: a direct line to the person working on your car, no franchise upsell quotas, and pricing that isn't inflated to cover corporate overhead.

Dealership & Chain Shops
What You Often Get
OEM markup on every part
Dealers charge OEM prices on parts that can be sourced for a fraction of the cost without sacrificing quality.
Service advisor in the middle
You rarely talk to the actual mechanic. A service advisor translates — and sometimes upsells — between you and the technician.
Monthly quotas and upsells
Chain shops run sales targets. Technicians are sometimes incentivized to find — or create — additional service needs.
Different technician every time
No continuity. Each visit starts from zero — nobody knows your car's history or what was found last time.
D's Auto Repair
What You Actually Get
Fair-market parts pricing
We source quality parts at honest prices and pass the savings to you. We'll explain every part on your invoice.
Talk directly to the mechanic
David and his team communicate with you directly. You get straight answers from the person who actually worked on your vehicle.
No quotas, no pressure
Independent shops succeed by retaining customers — not by squeezing every dollar from one visit. Honesty is the business model.
We remember your car
Return customers get a team that knows their vehicle's history. Brian and David keep track — so you don't have to explain everything again.
